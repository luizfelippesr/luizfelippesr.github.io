Impact of primordial magnetic fields on the formation of satellite galaxies

Thesis Companion resources Related links Author


Download


Authors:

Luiz Felippe S. Rodrigues, Carlton Baugh and Reuven Opher


Abstract

We suggest a new astrophysical mechanism that implies in a reduction of the discrepancy between observed and predicted properties for the satellite galaxies population. Using the Galacticus semi-analytic model of galaxy formation, we examine how primordial magnetic fields can affect the galaxy formation processes by adding an extra pressure component which acts preventing the gas accretion onto the dark matter haloes. For realistically small field values, we find significant reduction in the number of faint satellite galaxies, allowing a match between the predicted and observed luminosity function.
