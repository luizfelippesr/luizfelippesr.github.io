TILT, TWIST AND CURRENT HELICITY IN SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS VERSUS THEIR OBSERVATIONAL MANIFESTATIONS

Kirill Kuzanyan (IZMIRAN, Moscow, Russia) in collaboration with Nathan Kleeorin, Igor Rogachevskii (Israel), Andrei Tlatov (Kislovodsk, Russia), and Dmitry Sokoloff (Moscow, Russia)

Tilt of sunspots as well as current helicity and twist of magnetic fields in solar active region are believed to be important drivers of the solar dynamo mechanism. These quantities are measurable in the Sun already for several cycles, and used in mean field dynamo models for describe self-consistent evolution of helicity and magnetic fields. Using a basic model for convection on super-granulation scales below the solar photosphere we estimate the amount of tilt and twist (current helicity) of bipolar active regions on their rising phase due to Coriolis force, and compare these results with intrinsic twist (and helicity) of magnetic field from mean-field dynamo models. We also compare the results with available observations of tilt and current helicity from statistical studies of magnetograms of the solar photosphere. The results show that the both amounts of twist produced by Coriolis force and by dynamo models are of comparable order but different in sign. Therefore, they may compete, yielding in smaller active regions mainly the sign of helicity in accord with hemispheric sign rule (helicity and twist in Northern/Southern hemispheres of the Sun are predominantly negative/positive). For larger active regions the overall sign is probably reversed, so the same is expected for average tilts of bipolar groups. The most important property of the tilt is that it appears odd with latitude dependence with a small additional contribution which is even with latitude, so it is systematically different from zero level at the equator, depending on the phase of the solar cycle. These theoretical estimates are compared with available observations of tilt of bipolar active groups in solar photosphere.
